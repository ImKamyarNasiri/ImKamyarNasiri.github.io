About me

I am currently pursuing a Master of Science (M.Sc.) degree in Computer Science at Université Laval in Quebec City, working under the supervision of Prof. Philippe Giguère since May 2023. I earned my Bachelor of Science (B.Sc.) in Computer Engineering at Ferdowsi University of Mashhad, Iran. My research focuses on the photo-interpretation of drone and airborne images of Quebec's forests, with a particular emphasis on regrowth zones. This work aims to facilitate the tracking of these areas. I employ semi-supervised and self-supervised learning techniques for segmenting unlabelled and few labelled aerial images.

Broader interests

I am also interested in art, literature, philosophy, and math. I have a special place in my life for Persian poetry; growing up, I spent much time lost in the beautiful words of great Persian poets like Rumi. When I started my bachelor's degree, I got into the ideas of famous Western philosophers like Descartes and Kant. Moving to Canada was a significant change for me, but art helped me feel less lonely. Math has become something I'm curious about, mainly because it will be critical for the future of my research.
